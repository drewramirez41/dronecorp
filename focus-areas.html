Technical focus

Core research areas

DroneCorp. centers work on autonomy, perception, resilient networking, and data products that plug cleanly into existing command-and-control and analytics ecosystems. The emphasis is on applied R&D that can transition to programs of record without bespoke infrastructure.

Autonomy & edge processing

Onboard decision-making at the edge

Work in this area explores onboard perception, navigation assists, and mission-guardrails that keep operators in the loop while reducing cognitive load. Architectures favor modular, containerized components that can be re-targeted across multiple airframes and compute footprints.

Edge AI Navigation Human-in-the-loop Modular stacks
ISR payloads & sensing

Multi-sensor payload integration

Effort here focuses on fusing EO/IR, low-light, and situational sensors into payload packages that are maintainable and exportable. Interfaces are designed to present simple abstractions to C2 systems while preserving full-fidelity data for downstream analytics.

ISR EO / IR Fusion Exportable baselines
Comms & networking
Resilient mesh constructs

Research into low-SWaP networking that maintains mission continuity under contested or degraded conditions. Concepts emphasize mesh topologies, graceful degradation, and open interfaces to existing radios and waveforms.

Mesh Resilient comms Open API
Swarm behaviors
Cooperative autonomy at scale

Early-stage teaming logic for small, affordable vehicles operating as a coordinated system. Focus is on tasking, deconfliction, and operator tooling rather than cinematic demos.

Swarm Tasking Operator tools
Mapping & digital twin
Clean outputs for downstream tools

Tooling that converts raw collections into mapping and digital-twin products compatible with existing exploitation environments. Emphasis is on deterministic, well-documented data products rather than custom UIs.

Integration & sustainment
Interfaces that survive fielding

Work aimed at keeping systems maintainable once deployed: configuration management, logging, and interfaces that play well with existing DevSecOps pipelines and sustainment practices.
