Technical focus

Focus Areas

DroneCorp. is oriented around autonomy, perception, and integration work that connects unmanned platforms into existing command-and-control and analytics ecosystems. Focus areas below outline where early-stage teaming and research conversations are most relevant.

Autonomy & Edge AI
Onboard decision support

Lightweight autonomy stacks, assistive navigation, and perception pipelines tuned for constrained compute. Emphasis on explainable behaviors, tunable levels of human-in-the-loop control, and clean interfaces into higher-level mission planning tools.

Navigation & State Estimation
Challenged environments

Visual-inertial odometry, GPS-degraded navigation, and place-recognition approaches for interior and urban operations. Focused on robust state estimation that can be exposed via simple, documented APIs.

Communications & Networking
Swarm & mesh constructs

Resilient, low-bandwidth comms, mesh networking between vehicles, and efficient telemetry formats. Priority on working within existing radios and waveforms rather than bespoke hardware wherever possible.

Mission Systems & Tooling
Operator-centric workflows

Mission-planning UIs, replay and debrief tooling, and automation that reduces operator workload while keeping critical decisions transparent. Integration with existing C2, TDL, and analytics pipelines is favored over standalone tooling.

Test, Safety & Validation
Operational realism

Structured flight-test approaches, safety cases, and instrumentation to characterize autonomy performance. Emphasis on reproducible test harnesses and data products that partner teams can reuse.

Manufacturing & Supply Chain
U.S.-centric sourcing

Practical manufacturing paths for small-batch and low-rate production using a U.S.-leaning supply chain. Work includes design-for-manufacture considerations, documentation, and options for future scaling with qualified partners.
