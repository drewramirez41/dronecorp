Focus areas

Where the work is pointed

DroneCorp. is deliberately narrow. The emphasis is on autonomy-heavy ISR, honest telemetry, and manufacturing approaches that fit small teams and constrained budgets.

Autonomy

Perception & guidance

Lightweight autonomy stacks that hold up in real environments: cluttered maritime decks, urban alleys, indoor spaces, and ranges where RF conditions are not ideal.

Edge compute

Onboard processing

Experimentation with single-board computers, IMUs, cameras, and RF front-ends that can run useful logic locally, not only by calling out to cloud services.

Manufacturing

Small-batch hardware

US-based fabrication, assembly, and test flows that are realistic for a small team: 3D-printed fixtures, COTS-heavy builds, and repeatable procedures instead of bespoke one-offs.

Telemetry

Logs over marketing

Emphasis on robust logs, repeatable test runs, and introspection tools. If a flight goes bad, the stack should make it obvious why—not bury it.

Safety & constraints

Bounded experimentation

Test and development flows that respect airspace, export, and safety constraints from day one, not bolted on later under pressure.

Operator experience

Working with teams

Tooling, checklists, and UI that reflect how operators actually work—preflight routines, handoffs, and debriefs—instead of only optimizing slide decks.
