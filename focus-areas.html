Research vectors

Focus areas

Work is biased toward autonomy-heavy ISR concepts, resilient comms, and architectures that plug cleanly into existing mission-planning and analytics ecosystems. The goal is to de-risk integration, not add another silo.

Autonomy & edge AI

Onboard perception and navigation

Exploration of low-SWaP edge processing stacks for perception, navigation assists, and operator-in-the-loop decision support. Focused on explainable behavior and deterministic fallbacks rather than opaque black-box autonomy.

Edge compute Perception Assisted nav
Interior & confined-space ISR

Sub-terranean and structure recon

Concepts for navigating cluttered, GPS-denied interiors: industrial plants, ships, and underground spaces. Emphasis on mapping, low-light sensing, and safe maneuvering around humans and infrastructure.

GPS-denied Low light Digital twin
Maritime & littoral ISR

Carrier-adjacent and pier-side awareness

Surface and low-altitude sensing concepts intended to sit alongside existing naval assets. Integration with AIS, radar, and command displays comes first; visuals and dashboards follow.

Maritime Perimeter C2 hooks
Networking

Swarm, mesh, and backhaul

Research into small, resilient networks that allow multiple vehicles to cooperate without brittle dependence on a single link. Preference for open, inspectable interfaces over proprietary lock-in.

Mesh Swarm Open API
Manufacturing

U.S.-centric supply & repeatability

Process development for small-batch builds that can scale when programs require it. Priority on traceable sourcing, maintainability, and repeatable test regimes.

U.S. supply chain QA Test harness
Integration

C2, analytics, and mission tools

Everything is designed to be a good neighbor in existing stacks: STANAG-style feeds, clean APIs, and sensible data products that analysts can actually use.
