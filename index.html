R&D emphasis

Core focus areas

Activities are centered on technical exploration, prototyping, and integration rather than mass production. The guiding principle is a U.S.-centric supply chain with an emphasis on reliability, maintainability, and alignment with future autonomy frameworks.

Autonomy & Edge Processing

Onboard perception, navigation assists, and mission logic designed to offload operators while maintaining human authority over effects and outcomes.

Sensor & Payload Integration

Open-architecture payload bays intended to host EO/IR, mapping, and specialized mission packages without locking into a single vendor ecosystem.

Secure Link Concepts

Link concepts prioritizing resilience, low probability of intercept/detect, and compatibility with existing tactical networking constructs.

Manufacturability & Sustainment

Designs are evaluated with lifecycle cost, depot-level repair, and U.S.-based component sourcing in mind from the earliest phases.

Exploratory efforts

Representative internal initiatives

The following initiative names describe internal lines of effort and technology exploration. They are not associated with any specific government program of record.

PROJECT AERIS
Lightweight ISR airframes with emphasis on austere launch, recovery, and rapid tasking.
Focus: airframe studies, flight profiles, payload envelopes, operator workload reduction.
PROJECT NIGHTWATCH
Interior and low-visibility reconnaissance concepts for law-enforcement and defense users.
Focus: constrained spaces, autonomy assists, low-light sensing, deconfliction with ground teams.
PROJECT HARBORLINE
Perimeter monitoring and multi-domain awareness across air, ground, and maritime boundaries.
Focus: integration with existing security stacks, alerting workflows, and layered sensors.

Careers & collaborators

Early technical hire / collaborator

DroneCorp is in an early, pre-funding phase focused on design studies, prototyping, and proof-of-concept work. At this stage, the emphasis is on finding one highly capable technical generalist who can help shape the first generation of systems and tooling.

Founding Engineer – Unmanned Systems Prototyper

Hybrid role for a hands-on builder who is comfortable moving between CAD, firmware, low-level software, test rigs, and documentation. Ideal candidates enjoy working close to the hardware and are comfortable operating in an ambiguous, startup-style environment.

Representative skill areas (not all required):

  • Small UAS / robotics prototyping and basic mechanical design.
  • Embedded systems, microcontrollers, or companion computers on drones.
  • Basic autonomy / navigation stacks or integration with existing open-source stacks.
  • Sensor and radio integration (cameras, RF front-ends, telemetry links).
  • Ability to build and maintain clean test setups, logs, and simple internal tools.

This role may begin as part-time, contract, or internship-style collaboration while formal funding pathways are developed. U.S. persons only; this description is for exploratory interest and does not constitute a formal offer of employment.
