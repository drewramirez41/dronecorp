Engagement model

Programs & paths

DroneCorp. operates as an R&D-centric effort. Programs below describe typical ways we engage with primes, government sponsors, and technology partners—from early conversations through prototype deployments.

Technical dialogue sessions
0–90 days · discovery

Short, structured conversations focused on problem framing, constraints, and existing systems. Intended for primes and government teams who want to test fit with ongoing efforts without committing to a lab or integration sprint.

Outputs: high-level briefs, integration sketches, risk map.

Concept co-design sprints
3–6 months · lab work

Joint sprint cycles where teams refine a concept platform, autonomy stack, or integration pattern. Work typically includes requirements refinement, architecture options, and early hardware/software prototypes that can graduate into a sponsored effort.

Outputs: prototype, test plan, manufacturing notes.

Prototype & field experiments
6–18 months · deployment

Applied trials of unmanned concepts in controlled field environments. Emphasis on instrumentation, data products, and documentation that sponsors can carry forward into formal acquisition or larger scaled programs with existing integrators.

Outputs: flight reports, data products, handoff package.
