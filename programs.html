Programs

How engagements typically start

DroneCorp. is not a scaled prime. Most work begins as tightly scoped experiments: small sets of air vehicles, constrained test envelopes, and clear questions we want to answer together.

Phase I

Concept & problem shaping

High-level discussions around mission constraints, airspace realities, and what “useful” looks like. The outcome is a short, unclassified program note—not a 90-page deck.

Phase II

Prototype exercises

Build or adapt small batches of hardware for range or lab trials. Expect tight feedback loops, messy notes, and honest assessments of what actually happened in the air.

Phase III

Early racks & integration

Mature promising prototypes into repeatable racks: shared configs, scripted test runs, and telemetry that your own teams can interrogate without us in the loop.

Program descriptions are illustrative. Actual work will align with contracting, export, and security constraints.
