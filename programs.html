Program trajectory

Engagement models & pathways

DroneCorp. structures work so that concepts can move from lab experimentation to repeatable capability without bespoke infrastructure. Most efforts start as small, technically-focused projects and expand only when the underlying architecture is ready to support scale.

Typical path Concept studies → Lab & field experiments → Limited-scope pilots → Transition-ready capability paths
Applied autonomy experiments

Edge autonomy & perception trials

Small, tightly-scoped efforts focused on autonomy frameworks, onboard perception, and navigation assists. Typical work includes algorithm bring-up on representative hardware, closed-course testing, and data collection to inform future pilots.

R&D Edge AI Indoor & close-range ISR
Interior & maritime pilots

Confined-space & littoral ISR pilots

Early deployments of interior nano-UAS and surface ISR skiffs in controlled environments. Efforts emphasize operator workflows, safety envelopes, and integration with existing command-and-control and video systems.

Pilot Interior recon Maritime ISR
Integration & capability drops

Clean handoffs into existing stacks

Work centered on packaging autonomy, sensing, and networking capabilities into discrete drops that can be consumed by larger programs. Focus is on interfaces, observability, documentation, and sustaining engineering rather than custom front-ends.

Partnered programs

Teaming with primes & integrators

Cooperative efforts where DroneCorp. supplies focused autonomy or ISR components into a broader system architecture. Typical engagements involve shared roadmaps, interface control documents, and coordinated experiments rather than one-off demonstrations.
