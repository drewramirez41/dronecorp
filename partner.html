Teaming

Partner with DroneCorp.

DroneCorp. is built to slot into existing programs, not compete with them. The best fits are teams that want a nimble, U.S.-based builder focused on early racks, test articles, and integration work long before a large production award.

Where we fit

Typical engagements look more like a technical working group than a vendor relationship. We work best with partners who:

  • Have an existing customer program and need small-batch hardware or autonomy R&D.
  • Want clean integration into their C2, mapping, or analytics stack rather than a new UI.
  • Value transparent engineering tradeoffs over glossy marketing decks.

Prime contractors, niche subsystem providers, and government labs are all in scope. If there’s a shared problem worth attacking, we’re interested.

First conversations

Initial discussions are unclassified and high-level: problem space, constraints, and where you think a concept airframe or autonomy module might help. If it looks promising we can work out NDAs and more formal scopes.

The most useful things to bring to an intro call are:

  • One or two concrete mission scenarios you care about.
  • Any hard constraints: airspace, ship class, facility type, comms limitations.
  • Existing tools we’d need to integrate with (C2 suites, data formats, etc.).
