Careers

Build DroneCorp. from the zero line

DroneCorp. is an early-stage U.S. unmanned systems effort focused on autonomy-heavy ISR concepts, small-batch manufacturing, and defense-aligned R&D. We are not a scaled prime—we’re building toward that with a very small, high-trust team that wants to wire the first racks, write the first flight code, and sit in the first customer briefings.

Open roles

Early-stage hiring priorities

You’ve helped take at least one defense or dual-use hardware company from “interesting deck” to real programs of record, or you’ve built a manufacturing line that successfully delivered into defense supply chains.

  • Own the path from concept platforms to funded pilots, OTAs, and early production lots.
  • Design lean manufacturing and QA flows for small-batch ISR and autonomy-heavy platforms.
  • Stand up basic compliance posture (ITAR awareness, facility controls, vendor hygiene).
  • Translate customer problem sets into focused internal roadmaps and demo plans.

This is the closest thing DroneCorp. has to a co-founder track. You’ll be shaping what the company actually becomes.

You like integrating things: perception stacks, control code, radios, payloads, and ground tools into actual flying, rolling, or swimming hardware.

  • Own autonomy and edge-processing architectures on early concept platforms.
  • Prototype, integrate, and flight-test sensor packages and autonomy behaviors.
  • Work closely with manufacturing to keep designs realistic, testable, and supportable.
  • Help write the technical stories for proposals, whitepapers, and demo briefs.

This is the person who can wire the bench, assemble the airframes, run the checklists, and drag a generator into the desert at 0600 without drama.

  • Stand up lab and field test infrastructure for small UAS and related systems.
  • Own build logs, configuration control, and basic reliability tracking.
  • Coordinate range time, safety processes, and instrumentation for flight tests.
  • Provide grounded feedback into design decisions from a maintainer/operator perspective.

Internal title is “Chief Vibe Officer.” You’ve run a kitchen, café, or back-of-house operation, and you like building spaces where people actually want to spend long days shipping hard things.

  • Design and run a small in-house kitchen / snack program for test days and late nights.
  • Keep the shop and studio calm, stocked, and organized—tools, coffee, and music dialed in.
  • Coordinate small events, visiting partners, and demo-day logistics.
  • Be the unofficial morale barometer and reality check for the team.

Yes, this is a real role. We take food, environment, and morale seriously.
