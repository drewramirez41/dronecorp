Director of Defense Manufacturing & Programs
Tucson, AZ · Hybrid · Early leadership hire
Defense manufacturing DoD programs Scale-up
›

Role overview

This role is responsible for taking DroneCorp. from prototype hardware and experimental platforms to a disciplined, repeatable manufacturing and delivery posture suitable for defense and government work. You’ve helped build this arc before and are comfortable operating between the shop floor, engineers, and program sponsors.

What you'll do

  • Design and stand up a small but scalable manufacturing and integration environment for unmanned systems.
  • Define build processes, quality checks, and documentation suitable for defense customers.
  • Partner with engineering on platform baselines, configuration management, and change control.
  • Support capture and execution of early contracts, pilots, and test events.
  • Establish vendor and supply-chain relationships with a U.S.-centric bias.
  • Help shape roadmaps so technology and manufacturing maturity move in step.

Who you are

  • Have played a key role in scaling a defense or aerospace hardware effort from prototype to production.
  • Comfortable with DoD or government acquisition pathways (SBIR, OTA, IDIQ, PoR, etc.).
  • Fluent working with both engineers and program/business stakeholders.
  • Hands-on mindset: willing to walk the floor, debug, and iterate with a small team.
  • U.S. person; prior clearance or clearance-eligible is preferred for certain work.

Title and scope are flexible for the right person. This is intended as an early leadership hire with strong influence on how DroneCorp. grows.

Autonomy & Systems Integration Engineer
Tucson, AZ · On-site/Hybrid · First engineering hire
Autonomy Systems integration Hands-on
›

Role overview

This is a broad, hands-on engineering role focused on integrating autonomy stacks, payloads, radios, and compute into real unmanned platforms. You’ll work directly with the founder on bringing concepts to life and making sure they can be tested, iterated, and eventually handed to customers.

What you'll do

  • Integrate sensors, radios, and compute into air and surface platforms.
  • Support autonomy stack bring-up, tuning, and data collection.
  • Design test setups for indoor, range, and field evaluations.
  • Own wiring, mounts, and practical details that make systems shippable.
  • Help define platform baselines and maintain engineering documentation.
  • Collaborate on safety procedures and checklists for flight and ground tests.

Who you are

  • Comfortable working across hardware, firmware, and software boundaries.
  • Experience with unmanned systems, robotics, or embedded platforms.
  • Comfortable with lab tools, basic fabrication, and hardware debugging.
  • Enjoy building early versions, testing them hard, and iterating quickly.
  • U.S. person preferred; some work may eventually touch export-controlled domains.

Titles like “Senior Autonomy Engineer” or “Lead Systems Engineer” are also possible depending on background. This role is designed to be a cornerstone technical hire.

Operations Programs R&D support

Role overview

This role sits at the intersection of operations, light program management, and technical coordination. The focus is on keeping projects, vendors, documentation, and logistics moving so that engineers and leadership can stay focused on building and testing.

What you'll do

  • Track tasks, timelines, and deliverables across small R&D efforts and pilots.
  • Support basic contracting artifacts, SOW drafts, and vendor quotes.
  • Coordinate test events, travel, and logistics with partners and ranges.
  • Help maintain documentation, run books, and simple dashboards.
  • Assist with vendor onboarding, inventory, and reordering for key components.
  • Act as a central point of contact for “what’s happening where” inside the company.

Who you are

  • Comfortable working in technical environments, even if you’re not writing code daily.
  • Organized, detail-oriented, and calm under changing priorities.
  • Experience in operations, project coordination, defense, or technical programs is a plus.
  • Comfortable communicating with engineers, vendors, and customers.
  • U.S. person preferred; some work may eventually align with export or clearance contexts.

This role is intentionally broad: it is meant to absorb the operational load that accumulates as DroneCorp. spins up more experiments, partners, and programs.

Chief Vibes & Sustenance Officer (CVSO)
Tucson, AZ · On-site · Back-of-house + morale
Kitchen ops Morale Startup energy
›

Role overview

The Chief Vibes & Sustenance Officer is responsible for the two most fragile systems in any early-stage company: morale and snacks. This role treats the kitchen as a sustainment node and the common area as a low-friction operating environment.

What you'll do

  • Maintain snack readiness levels (SRL) at or above mission-acceptable thresholds.
  • Operate the DroneCorp. kitchen as a calm, efficient back-of-house environment.
  • Prepare simple but excellent food for small teams (e.g., wings, tacos, grilled cheese, breakfast runs).
  • Monitor ambient vibes and apply corrective measures (music, coffee, levity) as needed.
  • Help coordinate “we shipped something” cookouts and low-key team events.
  • Serve as an informal pulse check on burnout, late nights, and when people need a break.

Who you are

  • Have real back-of-house or kitchen experience, or can run one like you do.
  • Know at least one signature dish people still talk about later.
  • Can make coffee suitable for software engineers and field testers.
  • Comfortable in a shop/warehouse/office hybrid space.
  • Can read a room and adjust energy without making it about you.

This is intentionally written with a smile, but it is also a real role: startups run better when somebody owns hospitality, environment, and small rituals that keep people going.
